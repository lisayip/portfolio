STYROLOOP

In the UK we accumulate 25,000 tonnes of residual scrap polystyrene (EPS) each year in the form of packaging which is the equivalent to nearly 12,000 double decker London buses. According to Defra, currently 70% of household waste is going to incineration (EfW) and 30% is going to Landfill.

Next Project

Research

So far there is no infrastructure in the UK to cater for the disposal of EPS, councils do not classify the material as recyclable and there are few waste management companies that offer EPS recycling for businesses. We researched and ideated numerous flows to tackle this problem and design a viable circular solution to reduce single use plastic and Styroloop was formed.

Challenges

  • Few waste managemment offering recycling facilities
  • EPS is currently not recyclable
  • China ban of plastics to be recycled at end 2017
  • Confusing recycling legislation

Solutions

The concept supports businesses and the world at large through an innovative waste retrieval process that redirects and reduces the landfill of EPS. By collecting EPS and processing the material in a machine that takes the air bubbles out of EPS shrinking it to 0.1% of its size, we aim to reuse the raw material to design into useful products and repurposed back into the environment thus closing the loop and making EPS a sustainable product.
